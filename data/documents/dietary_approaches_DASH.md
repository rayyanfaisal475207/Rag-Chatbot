# Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension (DASH)

The DASH diet is a lifelong approach to healthy eating that is designed to help treat or prevent high blood pressure (hypertension). It was developed to lower blood pressure without medication in research sponsored by the National Institutes of Health.

## Core Principles
The DASH diet encourages you to reduce the sodium in your diet and eat a variety of foods rich in nutrients that help lower blood pressure, such as potassium, calcium and magnesium.

### Daily Serving Recommendations (Based on a 2000-calorie diet)
- **Grains:** 6 to 8 servings a day. Focus on whole grains like brown rice or quinoa.
- **Vegetables:** 4 to 5 servings a day. Tomatoes, carrots, broccoli, sweet potatoes.
- **Fruits:** 4 to 5 servings a day. Apples, bananas, berries.
- **Dairy:** 2 to 3 servings a day. Fat-free or low-fat milk or yogurt.
- **Lean meat, poultry and fish:** 6 one-ounce servings or fewer a day.
- **Nuts, seeds and legumes:** 4 to 5 servings a week. Almonds, kidney beans, lentils.
- **Fats and oils:** 2 to 3 servings a day.
- **Sweets and added sugars:** 5 servings or fewer a week.

## Sodium Limits
- **Standard DASH diet:** Up to 2,300 milligrams (mg) of sodium a day.
- **Lower sodium DASH diet:** Up to 1,500 mg of sodium a day (recommended for maximum blood pressure reduction).
