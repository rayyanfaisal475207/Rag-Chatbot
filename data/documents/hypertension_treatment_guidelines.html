Pharmacological Treatment of Hypertension

The selection of initial pharmacological agents for hypertension is critical for reducing cardiovascular risk.

First-Line Agents

  • Thiazide-type diuretics: Chlorthalidone (12.5-25 mg daily) is preferred due to prolonged half-life and proven trial reduction of CVD.
  • Calcium Channel Blockers (CCBs): Amlodipine (5-10 mg daily) is effective, particularly in Black patients and older adults.
  • ACE Inhibitors (ACEi): Lisinopril (10-40 mg daily) protects against nephropathy. Should not be used in combination with ARBs.
  • Angiotensin Receptor Blockers (ARBs): Losartan (50-100 mg daily) is an excellent alternative for patients who develop an ACEi-induced cough.

Special Populations

In patients with stable ischemic heart disease, beta-blockers (e.g., Metoprolol, Carvedilol) are indicated alongside an ACEi or ARB.

In patients with chronic kidney disease (CKD stage 3 or higher or albuminuria >300 mg/day), ACE inhibitors are the initial drug of choice to slow renal deterioration.
